Overview

Readimg is typically an individual activity, although on occasion a person reads out loud for other listerner, or reads aloud for for ones own use. for better comprehension. Before the reintroduction of separated text (space between words) in the late Middle Ages, the ability to read silently was considered rather remarkable.

Major predictors of an individual`s ability to read both alphabetic and non-alphabetic scripts are oral language skills phenological awareness, rapid automatized naming and verbal IQ

As a leisure activity, children and adult read because it is pleasant and interesting. in the US about half of all adult read one or more books for pleasure each year. About 5% read more than 50 books per year. American read more if they have education ,read fluently and easily, are female, live in cities and have higher socioeconomoic status. Children become better reader when they know more about the world in general and when they perceive reading as fun than another chore to be permitted.

Reading vs Literacy

Reading is an essential part of literacy yet from a historical perspertive literacy is about having the ability to both read and write.

And since the 1990s some organization have defined literacy in a variety of ways that may go beyond the traditional ability to read and write. the following are some examples

  • The ability to read and write in all media (print or electronic) including digital literacy
  • The ability to understand using printed and written material associated with varying contexts
  • The ability to read, write, speak and listen
  • Having the skill to be able to read, write and speak to understand and create meaning
  • The ability to communicate using visual, audible and digital materials
  • The ability to use printed and written information to function in society to achieve one`s goal and to one`s knowledge and potential.
